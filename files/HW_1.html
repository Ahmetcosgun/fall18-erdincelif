1. Task 1

The given data in this homework includes the match data and odd details files.

Match data includes the soccer game informations in English Premier League from 2011 to today. First, the format of the Unix Epoch date information is converted to date using âanytimeâ function. Then, game scores are splitted into two as home and away scores, and total game scores are obtained by adding those.

In odd details file, there are odds set by 27 bookmakers in different bet and odd types with corresponding date. There is also totalhandicap column representing bet for the total game score in matches. From the âodd detailsâ data.table, through the filterings through odd type, bet type and total handicap level of 2.5, first the data consisiting of the probability of the games with a total score higher than 2.5 is obtained. Then, over and under probabilities are normalized in a way that their sum will add up to 1.0.

Next, the merging the match and odd details tables in a way that for each match there are the odds with their corresponding match information is performed. The empirical and probabilistic and empirical over probabilities are calculated and the data is observed in bins of 0.05 with number of occurances (N).

1.1. Task 1.a

In this task, it is aimed to check the consistency of 5 different bookmakers (Pinnacle, bet365, bwin, ComeOn, Youwin) in setting their odds for over/under bets.

The graphs for the probabilistic over vs empirical over are plotted for 5 different bookmakers. Probabilisitic over represents the average of the standardized over odss in each bin and year (2010 - 2018). On the other hand, empirical over represents the ratio of the number of matches with a the total game score (total score higher than 2) to the total number of games in the corresponding bin and year. If a bookmaker were making the perfect estimations, it would have been expected to see all these probabilistic vs empirical overs on the y=x line.

First, the plot for bookmaker âPinnacleâ is drawn as in Figure 1. However, since all circles in the plot are not extracted from the same number of data points, the information on the importance of these circles are missing. Therefore, circle radius are drawn in such a way proportional to square root of the occurances in specific date-bin combination (N).

For all bookmakers same estimation trends (Figure 2 - 11) are observed. In the Figures with odd numbers, it is seen that there are deviations from the y=x line in the end regions (where empirical over is around 0 and 1). This can be explained with the fact that there are a few number of matches played in these area. Therefore, it is not reasonable to conclude that a bookmaker makes a bad estimation in those regions but shows that the matches played are ended unexpectedly. In order to increase the reliability of this analysis, in the Figures with even numbers, the data with less than 10 counts are removed.

It can be seen that in the range of bins 0.4 - 0.7, most of the odds are set by bookmakers and the estimations are quite consistent with the game results. For the bins in 0.2 - 0.4, bookmakers pay higher rewards for the bet players and this region can be categorized as risky due to its low match over probability. From the same viewpoint, in the region of 0.7 - 0.9 bins, bookmakers give lower rewards regarding the low risk on the game results.

2. Task 2

This task aims to practice big data visiualization. In R, ggplot2 package is one of the most efficient ways of performing such trivial visualization tasks.

It is known from the odd details data that a bookmaker changes the odds in time. The focus is on odds for match results, in other words bet type: 1x2. Regarding the more than 5 million instances in the data, certain filters are applied. The data is narrowed to bet type = 1x2, odd = odd1, bookmaker = Pinnacle, Year = 2017 which contains 12798 instances exactly.

Figure 16 is the plot for MatchId vs Odds. The information of the change in the odds is added via color gradient from blue to red implying first to last.

When the above plot is investigated, it is seen that there is no certain trend in the time varying odds meaning that there is no common trend for increase/decrease in the odd in time. If that were the case, the top-down color gradient would have been observed.

There is still some room to improve in this plot. Subgrouping the data regarding the number of odd changes (N) or months woulddecrease the datapoints in the plot and could give a better representation.
